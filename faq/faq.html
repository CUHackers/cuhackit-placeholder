FAQ

What is a hackathon?

A hackathon is a 24-hour invention marathon! Over the course of the event, teams of students will get together to design, build, and code their awesome new ideas and inventions!

What is CUhackit?

CUhackit is our annual nationwide hackathon in the spring semester, hosted virtually on Discord (and spiritually at Clemson University). Join our mailing list for more updates!

Who is CUhackit for?

If you're in college and are interested in making and/or learning something awesome, CUhackit is for you! This event is specifically designed to be a great experience for students at any level.

Why should I attend a hackathon?

First of all, it’s a ton of fun! Twenty-four hours with friends building cool projects, playing games, and competing for prizes--I mean, c’mon. It’s also a great chance to build out your resume with personal projects and interact with recruiters from companies like Amazon, Google, and more!

How do I participate?

The application will be released soon, but for now, check out our site's homepage if you want to join our mailing list.

What can I create?

The sky's the limit! In the past, we’ve had shop-vac bottle organs, freestyle rap generators, recycling robots, and virtual reality video games. You can create anything you can put your mind to!

How much does it cost to participate?

Absolutely free -- We are paying you to be here (Not really, but you get free stuff!)

What conduct is expected at CUhackit?

To keep CUhackit fun, fair, and welcoming for everyone, all participants are required to abide by the MLH Code of Conduct.
